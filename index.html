How to Make Snowflake Crisp with Maltose and Granola

Ingredients

  • 200g maltose
  • 75g sugar
  • 50ml water
  • pinch of salt
  • 30g butter
  • 1 egg white
  • 50g milk powder
  • 200g granola
  • 100g meat floss
  • 30g cheese powder
  • milk powder for decoration

Instructions


1Put 200g of maltose in the microwave oven for 15 seconds, this step is to make the maltose flow better.

2Pour the heated maltose into the pot, then add 75g of sugar, 50ml of water, a pinch of salt, and start boiling the syrup over medium-low heat, put a thermometer in the pot to control the temperature, the thermometer should not directly touch the bottom of the pot Oh, it will not be accurate. (Add sugar to reduce the water content of the syrup, so that it can boil faster to the right temperature is not easy to scorch. (And boiling sugar with the pot must be thick-bottomed Oh, otherwise it will be unevenly heated paste off.

3Put 200g of cereal, 100g of meat floss, 30g of cheese powder in a bowl, mix well.

4Melt 30g of butter in the microwave.

5Sift a layer of milk powder into a non-stick square baking dish.

6When the syrup boils to about 110℃, add an egg white and whip it at medium speed until stiff peaks form, keep an eye on the syrup temperature while whipping the egg, it will take about 5 minutes to heat up from 110℃ to 120℃, which is basically the time when the egg white is almost whipped.

7Quickly pour the syrup into the whipped egg whites, continuing to whip the syrup as you pour.

8Add the freshly melted butter and beat well.

9Quickly add 50g of milk powder while it is still hot and mix well.

10Then quickly add the meat loaf cereal and rub the cereal into the meringue with your hands, wearing gloves.

11Put the cereal syrup dough into the baking pan that has just been sifted with milk powder, push it hard with your hands while it is still warm and not completely set, using the sides of the baking pan to form a neat shape, and finally you can use a spatula to level the surface.

12Finally, sieve a layer of milk powder on the surface, and when it is thoroughly cooled, cut into small pieces on it. (Be sure to let it cool completely before cutting, otherwise it is easy to deform.)
